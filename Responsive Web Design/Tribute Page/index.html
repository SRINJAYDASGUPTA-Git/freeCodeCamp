Dr. J. Robert Oppenheimer

The Father of the Atomic Bomb

Here's a time line of Dr. Julius Robert Oppenheimer:

  • April 22, 1904 - Born in New York City, U.S
  • 1922 - Oppenheimer enrolls at Harvard University to study chemistry and physics.
  • 1925 - He travels to Europe to continue his studies, attending the University of Cambridge and the University of Göttingen, where he works under prominent physicists Max Born and Wolfgang Pauli.
  • 1927 - Oppenheimer receives his Ph.D. in physics from the University of Göttingen
  • 1929 - He returns to the United States and becomes a professor of physics at the University of California, Berkeley.
  • 1942 - The Manhattan Project is officially launched, and Oppenheimer is appointed as the scientific director, overseeing the development of the atomic bomb.
  • July 16, 1945 - The first successful test, codenamed 'Trinity' of an atomic bomb takes place in the desert of New Mexico. Oppenheimer famously quotes the Bhagavad Gita, saying, "Now I am become Death, the destroyer of worlds."
  • August 6 and 9, 1945 - The United States drops atomic bombs on the cities of Hiroshima and Nagasaki in Japan, leading to the end of World War II.
  • 1947 - Oppenheimer becomes the director of the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton, New Jersey.
  • 1953 - Amidst allegations of communist sympathies and concerns about his political leanings, Oppenheimer's security clearance is revoked by the Atomic Energy Commission.
  • 1963 - President Lyndon B. Johnson presents Oppenheimer with the Enrico Fermi Award for his contributions to science.
  • February 18, 1967 - J. Robert Oppenheimer passes away at the age of 62 in Princeton, New Jersey.

"He was the most intelligent man I ever met, but there was more than mere intelligence to his gift. There was also instinct, a feel for things. This is hard to speak about, for these are not elements which can be measured, nor even convincingly described."

-- Isidor Isaac Rabi, Nobel laureate in physics
